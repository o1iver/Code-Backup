Five children and their parents, supported by a team of educators, scientists and seamen spent two years to visit the most remote islands of the planet and learned how to explore, understand, communicate, and to grasp the beauty, diversity, harmony and challenges of nature and mankind.

Five children, two girls and three boys, and their Greek mother and German father departed on board of the 130 foot high performance sailing yacht Alithia for two school years to meet other children and families around the globe. They experienced the authenticity and comprehensiveness of the mi-crocosms “island”, the desire of isolation and of integration, the cultural diver-sity and environmental integrity of remote communities, the hardship and harmony that human societies and natural environments in isolated parts of this otherwise globalizing world have preserved. They brought into the remote communities their own young and open minds, their curiosity and creativity, the respect and honesty that children in all parts of the world share. They were great ambassadors.

The team of the voyage was diverse. The children and parents speak English, French, German and Greek. The educators came from France, Germany and Switzerland, the crew from Britain, New Zealand and Philippines. They lived on board the purpose built yacht providing the living quarters, the working, educational and medical facilities. The vessel maintains an advanced audio-visual and multimedia infrastructure, broad band satellite connection, scuba-diving equipment for underwater exploration, two match race laser boats, two tenders, fishing and water toys.

The children accomplished during the week day mornings their school cur-riculum with the French Distance Learning System CNED enriched by Ger-man teaching modules. In the afternoons and weekends they visited local villages, the schools, churches and homes to gain and exchange precious insights, experience common values, learn to respect diversity, feel the spiri-tual life and finally to build unforgettable ties. With rangers or local youth they discovered amazing nature and wild life. They discovered the different eco-systems, their equilibrium, their potential peril by human exploitation and their protection by human ingenuity.

It all is engraved in life long memories and documented in daily logbook en-tries, thousands of photos, reports, poems, songs and many presents.

The route led to Galapagos, Eastern Island, Mangareva (Isles Gambiers), Australes, Iles de la Societe, Penrhyn, Samoa, Tonga, Lau Islands, New Zealand and Noumea (New Caledonia), Iles de Pins, Loyalty Islands, Vanu-atu, Torres, Santa Cruz and Solomon Islands, Australia, Kupang, Komodo and Bali, Phuket, Mergui archipelago, Cheduba Islands and Sittwe in Myan-mar, Andaman Islands, Maldives, Chagos, Aldabra and Cosmeldo Group, Seychelles and finally through the Suez Canal to the Athens Olympics.

Five years ago the project was started. With pride the family looks back to an achievement that few dare; taking an entire family on such an epic voyage around the world. The oldest daughter made the thoughtful distinction be-tween dreaming a dream and living a dream. The dream gives the liberty to let the fantasy wonder. It lets imagine the unthinkable. Can one create a fam-ily bonded together by the same values: a spirit if freedom, a sense of taking responsibility of one's own life, a dedication to the life in community; one can.

And then living of the dream gives the perspective of truth, of the reality. It lets reach out for the opportunity and lets experience the limits. Children and parents saw nature and culture with all their charm and seduction. They saw poverty, ignorance and destruction. They were guests in worlds so strange and foreign to them. Worlds so diverse that have in common only that they are on the path to distinction. Everywhere they were received with open arm. Often they separated with tears.
